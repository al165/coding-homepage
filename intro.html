A very brief history

The Jacquard Loom

first reprogrammable machine that codified textile designs on punchcards

Ada Lovelace

- wrote the first ever computer program in 1842
- "The Analytical Engine weaves algebraic patterns just as the Jacquard loom weaves flowers and leaves."

Alan Turing

- designed one of the first electronic computers during WWII
- mathematically formulised what a computer program is
- regarded as the father of artificial intelligence

computers the size of rooms

early computer programs were made by connecting cables to control the flow of data

and punch cards remained the coding method until the mid-70s

even weaving became a method of coding!

the guidance computers of the Apollo Moon Landing was coded by weaving electrical wires in a very precise way

eventually, coding became a language with words...

Since then, computers have become quite a big deal...
